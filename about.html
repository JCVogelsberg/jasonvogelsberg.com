My Story

I'm J. Vogelsberg, a web developer living in Portland, Oregon. Originally from Louisville, Kentucky, I have spent the last decade living in Florida. Enjoying a wide range of interests, I started out as a journalism major, then switched to an Aviation Maintenance degree, graduating from Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in 2009. I worked in Light Sport aviation, helicopters, and Part-161 flight training operations before deciding I wanted a new technical challenge. This brought me first to web development, then to Oregon.

In 2014, I graduated from Epicodus, an intensive, four-month web development bootcamp, where I studied HTML, CSS, Javascript/jQuery, Ruby/Rails, PostgreSQL, and Ember.js. Test-driven development was practiced throughout, using Mocha/Chai, Rspec, and Capybara. All work was done in pairs or small groups, and Github was used for version control.

Since leaving Epicodus, I have completed the Code Oregon's Front End Web Development track.

When I'm not messing around in front of my MacBook, I enjoy building and rallying Hondas and Subarus, reading, archery, drinking ales, drinking tea, artisanal ballistics, writing, studying history, and sailing.
